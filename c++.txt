{Bitwise operations}-
    *Bitwise operations is the operations performed on binary numbers.
    *The <logical AND> of binary values is known as <bitwise AND>. It is because we are performing a bitwise operation of logical 
        AND of the given binary values.
    *Bitwise operations have different symbols for their function. Like the <bitwise AND> is denoted as (&)- except (&&). And 
        the <bitwise OR> is denoted as (|) except (||).
    *If we enter any and perform bitwise operations then first the values are converted into their binary form and then the main
        operations is performed.
    (Some of the following bitwise operators)-
        <bitwise &>(bitwise AND)
        <bitwise |>(bitwise OR)
        <bitwise ^>(exclusive XOR)
        <bitwise << >(bitwise left shift)
        <bitwise >> >(bitwise right shift)

    *<bitwise ^> is not known as bitwise XOR, rather it is known as <exclusive XOR>. In this type of operation, we say-
        (0 ^ 0)= 0 and (1 ^ 1)= 0
        (0 ^ 1)= 1 and (1 ^ 0)= 1
            Means if there are same values, then only the resultent value would be (0) and if there are no same values then the 
            resultent value would (1).
    *<bitwise << > This operator results in the shift of the bits of binary value to i times of a binary value n to their left 
                        side, by i th places.
                    *This bitwise operator does a special function at memory. It follows the calculation of (a * 2^b). Here, 
                        (a) is the decimal number that we give and (b) is the number of places that should be shifted to the left-
                        listed in numbers. The following expression results is the final value that a bitwise left shift operator
                        allocates at the memory.
    *<bitwise >> > This operator results in the shift of the bits of binary value to i times of a binary value n to their right 
                        side, by i th places.
                    *This bitwise operator also does a special function at memory. It follows the calculation of (a / 2^b). Here, 
                        (a) is the decimal number that we give and (b) is the number of places that should be shifted to the right-
                        listed in numbers. The following expression results is the final value that a bitwise left shift operator 
                        allocates at the memory.
